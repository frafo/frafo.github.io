Miniaturized PET extension for MRI devices

Harnessing the potential of new, magnetic field permeable electronics

Based on science and technology developed in the last 5 years, we research the feasibility of a miniaturized, modular positron emission tomography device. This new approach breaks the trade-off between cost, size and magnetic field compatibility through multidisciplinary engineering solutions from material and telecommunication science. The new device is bound to revolutionize the way PET is perceived by adapting it as an accessory of existing Magnetic Resonance Imagers, creating a groundbreaking multimodal imaging solution.

Micro-plastic open sea mapping

Independent networked marine detector

Due to the significant need to map and evaluate the amount and type of microplastics in seawater, the independent networked marine detector sailed out. Hosted within a wave-and-sun-powered skiff, a mini spectroscope controlled by an independent microcontroller provides water quality and GPS information on a periodical basis, allowing our backend tools to create a detailed map of micro-plastic pollution and water quality.

Hobby: water quality

Cost-effective water quality detector

A lite version of our marine detector, adapted for urban users, provides live water quality measurements and alerts and suggests remedies for the security and upkeep of hobby aquariums. The first of a series of micro-sustainability detecting systems, the HWQ system is a ground-breaking cost-effective alternative to existing solutions that simplifies the use and maintenance of hobbyist aquariums.
